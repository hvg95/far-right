Far-Right Political Parties on the Rise Throughout Europe

The recent successful of Geert Wilder's PVV party in Dutch parliamentary elections signals a growing shift to the right throughout Europe.

By Hannah Glaser

The unexpected parliamentary victories for the Dutch far-right political party Party for Freedom, or PVV, add to growing popularity for various far-right parties throughout European countries. Though many expressed shock at the sweeping victory, election data indicates a recent trend towards the far-right over the past ten years.

Information compiled from the ParlGov Project and PopList 3.0 datasets shows that, opposed to parliamentary elections ten years ago, far-right parties are gaining an increasing share of the total votes in several countries.

Countries whose far-right parties enjoyed a greater degree of success in the most recent elections, as opposed to ten years ago, include Italy, Sweden, Poland, and Croatia.

Several countries saw victories from far-right political parties that were either newly formed, or failed to win any of the total vote share in the previous elections. These include Germany, Spain, and Portugal.
